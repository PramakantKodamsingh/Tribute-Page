About Albert Einstein

Albert Einstein was a theoretical physicist who developed the theory of relativity, one of the two pillars of modern physics. His work is also known for its influence on the philosophy of science. Einstein is best known to the general public for his mass–energy equivalence formula E = mc².

Achievements

  • Developed the theory of relativity
  • Nobel Prize in Physics (1921)
  • Contributions to quantum mechanics and statistical mechanics
  • Philosophical works on science and technology

“Life is like riding a bicycle. To keep your balance you must keep moving.” - Albert Einstein
